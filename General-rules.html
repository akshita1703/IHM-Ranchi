General Rules
As you are joining IHM Ranchi Family, you are expected to:

Rules and Regulations

  • Comply with the rules and regulations of NCHMCT & IHM Ranchi which are issued or will be issued from time to time.
  • Conduct yourself in a congenial and respectable manner in your transactions with faculty, staff members, and fellow students.
  • Protect and preserve the environment and resources of IHM Ranchi.
  • Accept responsibility for jobs assigned to you during the process of learning.
  • Participate in academic and extracurricular activities both individually and in groups. Your participation will always be appreciated and rewarded.

The rules and regulations are essential for creating a cohesive environment, developing personality, skills, and a positive professional attitude towards the hospitality and service sector.

Personal Appearance and Self-Presentation

To maintain uniformity, discipline, and a professional interface with the industry, the following dress code and grooming guidelines are mandatory:

For Boys:

  • Wear the General Uniform: Black Trousers, White Full-Sleeve Shirt, and Uniform Tie.
  • Jeans and T-shirts are not allowed, even if there are no classes.
  • Always keep shirts tucked in and do not roll up sleeves.
  • Wear formal shoes (Oxford black) and black socks.
  • Ensure tattoos are not visible.
  • Do not wear ornaments like earrings or bangles, except for religious purposes.
  • Any kind of hair dye and spiked hairstyles are strictly prohibited.
  • Final-year students should wear a black coat in winter; 1st and 2nd-year students should wear a V-neck black pullover without any design.
  • Wearing a name plate during college hours is mandatory.

For Girls:

  • Wear the General Uniform: Black Trousers, White Full-Sleeve Shirt, and Black Scarf.
  • Jeans and T-shirts are not allowed, even if there are no classes.
  • Always keep shirts tucked in and do not roll up sleeves.

Important: Observing the dress and grooming code is mandatory for all students, even during vacation visits or official work deputation. Failure to comply will result in strict disciplinary actions. For clarification, students can meet the HODs or Principal.

Grooming & Hygiene

Boys

  • Clean, neatly styled hair. Short and well-combed.
  • Sides should not cover the ears. No long side locks.
  • Non-oily appearance. Use of hair gels, hair color, or bleached hair is not permitted.
  • Sikh students should maintain their hair and beard and wear a turban at all times.
  • Neatly trimmed nails.

Girls

  • Clean, neatly styled hair. Short hair must be styled away from the face.
  • Long hair should be tied in a bun, ponytail, or plait.
  • Conservative black pins, ribbons, and hairbands must be used to keep hair in place.
  • Non-oily appearance. Use of hair color, bleached hair is not permitted.
  • Neatly trimmed nails. Ladies using nail polish must ensure it is well-maintained and not chipped. Nail polish is prohibited in Food Production class.

Accessories - Don'ts

  • Expensive and showy watches.
  • Piercing of earlobes or visible tattoo marks.
  • Use of rings, chains, bracelets, or mobile phones inside the campus.
  • Flashy earrings and nose pins, or use of Henna (for girls).
  • Too much jewelry or strong perfumes.

Personal Hygiene Do's & Don'ts

Due to the physical nature of work, it is important that students take extra care of their health and hygiene. Personal hygiene is a critical aspect of personality and appearance, especially as food handlers.

  • Take a bath at least once a day, and twice during summer months.
  • Prevent any kind of body, mouth, socks, or sweat odor.
  • Male students must shave daily. No goatees or French beards are permitted unless for religious reasons, with prior approval.

Library & Internet

The library is a treasure for the student fraternity. Students must adhere to the rules to ensure convenience for all.

  • Library cards are issued after joining and are valid for six semesters. It is mandatory to enter the library or issue books.
  • Library operates from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM on working days (Lunch break: 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM).
  • Books should be returned within the stipulated period. Fines apply for late returns.
  • Students must pay for damaged or lost books (current market price + fines).
  • Rare and costly books cannot be issued but may be used for reference in the reading room.
  • Internet facilities are available for use during free periods or library hours.
  • Maintain decorum and silence. Eating, mobile phones, gossiping, and loitering are prohibited.
  • Follow the library notice board for updates.

Computer Lab

  • Internet facilities are available in the computer lab for students.
  • Silence must be maintained at all times in the computer lab.
  • If there is any query, contact the lab in-charge or concerned faculty member during lab classes.
  • No eatables and mobile phones are allowed inside the computer lab.
  • Students will be given Login IDs to access data and the Internet.
  • Playing games, viewing obscene sites, or chatting will lead to expulsion from the computer lab and disciplinary action.

Identity Card

  • Identity cards will be issued to all students at the time of joining and will be valid for the entire course duration.
  • Students must possess their I-card at all times while on campus premises.
  • I-cards must be shown to the guard at entry or on request. Failure to produce the card may attract disciplinary action.
  • Loss of I-card must be reported immediately to the institute and the local police. A duplicate I-card will be issued upon presenting a police diary and paying the applicable fine.

Notice & Information

  • Notice boards are placed at various locations in the campus for updates on academics, exams, training, holidays, and activities.
  • Both digital and hard notice boards are used to circulate information. Students should check notice boards daily for updates.
  • Guardians are informed of their ward's performance, attendance, and conduct through email or phone. Contact details must be registered during admission.
  • Students must inform the institute of any address changes for updated records and communication.
  • Important updates like vacations, timetables, exam schedules, and attendance are also published on the institute's website.
  • Parents may contact the institute for queries on working days between 9:00 AM and 5:30 PM. In emergencies, parents may be called beyond these hours.

Schedule of Payment of Term Fees

  • Fees must be paid before the semester commences. Payment can be made online or through other channels listed on the institute's website.
  • Late payments will incur penalties per day as late fee charges.
  • If fees are not paid by the due date, the student’s name will be removed from the class register. Readmission charges will apply to regain enrollment.

Clearance

All students must obtain a No Due Certificate (N.O.C.) before the term-end examination to receive their admit cards. N.O.C. requires:

  • Returning library books and cards to the library in-charge.
  • Paying any outstanding dues and fines for mishandling equipment or breakages.
  • Submitting the Log-book required for industrial exposure.
  • Vacating lockers and settling any fines for indiscipline.

General Guidelines

  • Institute working hours are 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM, five days a week.
  • Students must carry their identity card at all times.
  • Absence from any Institute activity without prior approval from the Principal is prohibited.
  • Smoking and drinking are strictly prohibited on campus.
  • Any breach of discipline may result in the forfeiture of fees paid and disciplinary action.
  • Students must notify the institute of any changes to their address for records.

Noise and Activity

Students must maintain absolute silence during class hours, in the library, restrooms, corridors, and public areas. Violations will be treated as indiscipline and attract penalties.

Institute Property

  • Maintaining and safeguarding the Institute's property is a top priority for students.
  • Any disfigurement or damage to property will result in fines and strict disciplinary action.
  • Graffiti or damage to walls in labs, classrooms, toilets, or public areas is strictly prohibited.

Locker Rooms & Toilets

  • Lockers are allotted to individual students. Sharing lockers is not permitted for security reasons.
  • Damage or tampering with lockers, walls, or public areas will result in fines and disciplinary action.

Loss of Property

Students are discouraged from bringing valuable items to campus. The Institute will not be responsible for any loss or damage to student property. Students must safeguard their belongings in their lockers.

Use of Mobile Phones

Mobile phones are strictly prohibited during working hours and are not allowed in lecture halls, labs, computer labs, and the library. Violations will result in confiscation of the phone without explanation.

Disciplinary Action

Violations of good conduct and ethical behavior are categorized into three parts. Punishments will vary depending on the severity of the offense.

Major Offences

  • Ragging of junior students.
  • Consumption of alcohol/drugs.
  • Criminal assault on any person inside or outside the Institute that warrants a police report (lodged or not).
  • Molestation of another person (student, staff, or any other individual).
  • Attempt to molest or sexual harassment in the form of indecent remarks.
  • Indecently attempting to touch a student or any person of the same or opposite sex.
  • Bullying someone individually or in a group.
  • Going out of the campus without permission or returning from leave/night out later than the prescribed time for unduly long periods.
  • Smoking, as it is prohibited for students inside the campus.
  • Misusing the Institute's email network for sending junk mail, offensive, harassing, or illegal communications to others.
  • Cheating or plagiarism in presentations, evaluation tests, and examinations.
  • Causing serious injuries to another person deliberately.
  • Theft or severe damage to another's property or Institute property, including electronic passwords, private information, etc.

Any of the above or other offences notified by the Institute as major may lead to immediate suspension. Parents/guardians will be notified and an inquiry will be conducted. Punishment may include rustication or dismissal if the student is found guilty.

Minor Offences

  • Failing to report to assigned duties or activities.
  • Using abusive language, which is considered unbecoming of a person in the Institute.
  • Shouting at or blatantly disobeying instructions from faculty or authorities.
  • Causing minor injuries to another person deliberately.
  • Leaving the campus without permission or failing to return within the prescribed time for small violations.
  • Appearing in attire or with an appearance that violates the prescribed rules.
  • Disobeying any rules or codes of conduct outlined in this handbook.
  • Any misconduct or wrongful act unbecoming of a student of the Institute of Hospitality Management.

Punishment for minor offences may include warnings, suspension, fines, or calling guardians. Repeated offences may lead to more severe consequences. If a student persistently commits minor offences and does not improve, the Principal may impose punishment for major offences without further warnings.
