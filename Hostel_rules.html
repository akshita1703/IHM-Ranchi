Hostel Accommodation Rules

Student Accommodation

IHM Ranchi provides accommodation for students in either the gentlemen's or the ladies' hostel. The charges for the room and surcharge will be notified from time to time.

Cleaning and Tidiness of Hostel Rooms

Hostel rooms must be cleaned properly at least once a week, and bed linen should be cleaned twice a week or as required. As part of the housekeeping training, students are required to clean their rooms in all respects. Corridors and common areas in the hostels and academic block will be cleaned by the Institute housekeeping staff.

Waste and Rubbish

All waste must be placed in bags, tied when full, and deposited in designated containers. Waste should not be placed in corridors, thrown out of windows, or stored on window ledges.

Storage of Luggage

Students can bring personal belongings in suitcases and bags. All items must be stored in cupboards or bed drawers, except for a small bag. At the end of each session or in cases like rustication, all belongings must be removed.

Bed Linen

The Institute does not provide bed linen. Students must bring their own bed linen, pillows, towels, and toiletries. All items should be marked with black indelible ink, including the student's name and room number, to avoid misplacement or disputes.

Electrical Gadgets

No TVs or other electrical gadgets are allowed in hostel rooms, except for laptops, music systems, and heaters during winter. Students may bring personal items like mugs, flasks, and water gadgets.

Important Guidelines

  • Students must be inside the campus before 8:00 PM and in their rooms by 10:00 PM (Sunday to Friday). No TV or games are allowed in common areas after this time.
  • Permission for a night out on Saturdays must be obtained from the hostel warden. Weeknight outings require exceptional reasons and Principal/Warden approval.
  • Loud noises, music, or disturbances are prohibited. Students must respect requests from peers or staff to minimize noise.
  • Noise must be kept to a minimum throughout the campus, especially between 10:00 PM and 7:00 AM. Students returning after 10:00 PM (e.g., from ODC) must do so quietly.

Food

Hosteller students are entitled to have all four meals (breakfast, lunch, evening tea, and dinner) on Saturday, Sunday, and holidays during the academic term in the mess. During weekdays, breakfast and dinner will be provided in the hostel mess. Lunch will be provided to all students (including hostellers) in the Institute. There are no charges for the same. Meal timings will be notified and must be adhered to.

Family Members & Visitors

The Institute encourages parents and family members to visit. However, they are requested to adhere to certain timings to meet the students to avoid interference with the academic schedule. Parents and family members are welcome to eat in the mess on fixed charges, provided prior information is given to the mess manager, if possible. Entry into hostel rooms will require permission from the warden or, in their absence, the Principal.

Medical Assistance: The Institute makes arrangements for minor ailments like colds and headaches. For major treatments, parents will be informed to take necessary action.

Note: The Institute reserves the right to alter or change the above guidelines, which will be binding on all students.

Refund of Hostel Fees

  • Within 1 month: ₹25,000
  • Within 2 months: ₹20,000
  • Note: If a student withdraws on any date within a month, the full monthly charge of ₹5,000 will apply.

Prior approval from the warden and Principal is mandatory before leaving the hostel.

Hostel Rules

  • Eligible students (as per the NCHMCT list) must clear all hostel fees as per the IHMCTAN Ranchi guidelines.
  • Required documents for hostel room allotment:
    • College allotment certificate
    • Hostel allotment certificate
    • 2 recent passport-size color photos
    • Copy of address proof (government provided)
    • Copy of Photo ID (government provided)
    • Copy of parent/guardian address proof
    • Copy of parent/guardian Photo ID
  • Room allotment is handled by hostel authorities only. Requests for specific room selection will not be entertained.
  • Rooms will be thoroughly checked upon release (at the end of even semesters). Any damages must be compensated by the hosteller.
  • Parents/guardians must register their contact number and email ID for official communications. Only registered details will be used for permissions (e.g., night-outs).
  • Hostel seats will be allotted on a first-come, first-serve basis.
  • Students expelled on disciplinary grounds will not be eligible for future hostel allotments.
  • The allotment list will be published on the college website and notice board 7 days prior to the semester's start.

General Rules

The college starts at 9:00 AM, and students are expected to reach class in complete uniform as per the timetable. They may return to the hostel during lunch hours to change uniforms.

  • Students must register in the IN-OUT Register after college hours if leaving the campus and return by 7:00 PM.
  • Attendance at 8:00 PM is mandatory for all hostellers. Missing it will be treated as a serious violation.
  • The hostel gate will be locked at 10:00 PM. Common rooms close at 11:00 PM.
  • Visiting fellow hostellers' rooms is not allowed between 11:30 PM and 6:00 AM.
  • Meals are provided at specific times:
    • Breakfast: 8:00 AM - 8:30 AM
    • Lunch: 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM
    • Evening Tea: 5:30 PM - 6:00 PM
    • Dinner: 8:00 PM - 9:00 PM
  • Hostellers cannot stay in their rooms during college hours unless they have reported illness and obtained permission from the warden/Hostel AO.
  • Night-out permissions require prior forms and SMS confirmation from parents (if applicable).

Civic & Safety Rules

  • Rooms must be kept clean. Unhygienic conditions may result in fines or rustication.
  • The campus is a Plastic-Free Zone. Dispose of plastic properly in designated bins.
  • Hostellers must have a personal dry waste bin cleared daily.
  • Room maintenance issues should be reported to hostel authorities. Students must not attempt repairs themselves.
  • Valuables should be stored securely. The hostel is not liable for any loss or damage.
  • Electrical gadgets like irons and immersion heaters are prohibited. Permitted devices include phones, laptops, and mosquito repellents.
  • TVs and music systems are not allowed. Music can only be played with earphones.

Disciplinary Issues

  • Ragging: A cognizable offense per the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India. The college has a ZERO TOLERANCE policy. Violations will result in strict punishment.
  • Possession or consumption of alcohol/narcotics is strictly prohibited. Students found intoxicated may face expulsion or suspension.
  • Smoking is prohibited in public areas and corridors.
  • Damaging hostel or institute property is a serious crime and may result in fines, suspension, or rustication.
  • Misbehavior with fellow students or staff will not be tolerated.
  • Visitors are not allowed in hostel rooms. Meetings should be held in designated common areas.
